# Finding the Right Tools

Not every problem needs the shiniest solution.

I've spent a lot of time watching people (including myself) pick tools based on hype rather than need. A framework because it's popular, a language because it's trending, a service because everyone uses it.

The best tools are the transparent ones. They solve the problem and then get out of the way.

## Questions Worth Asking

- Does this actually make my work easier, or just different?
- What am I giving up by adopting this?
- What's the cost when things break?
- Can I understand how it works?

## What I've Learned

Sometimes the oldest solution is the best one. HTML is still here. SQL hasn't gone anywhere. Unix philosophy—do one thing and do it well—still applies.

The tools that have lasted are the ones that respect your time and don't demand constant learning.
